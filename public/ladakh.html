Thukpa: Steaming bowls of comfort, warming the soul in every slurp.

Facts

Fact 1: Tibetan Roots: Thukpa, a beloved noodle soup, originates from Tibetan cuisine and has expanded its influence across the Himalayan regions, becoming a staple in Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of India.

Fact 2: Cultural Comfort Food: Beyond its culinary appeal, Rajma Chawal represents a cultural comfort food, often associated with familial gatherings, simplicity, and the warmth of home-cooked meals.

Fact 3: Nourishing Tradition: Historically, Thukpa served as a hearty meal for Himalayan communities, offering warmth and sustenance in cold climates, showcasing its historical significance as a nourishing comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams noodles (preferably flat or egg noodles)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup spinach or bok choy, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Recipe

  • Prepare Noodles: Cook the noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a large pot or pan. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  • Add Vegetables: Add sliced onions, julienned carrots, sliced bell pepper, and shredded cabbage. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  • Broth and Seasoning: Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth. Add soy sauce and chili sauce (if using). Bring it to a simmer and let it cook for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  • Combine Noodles and Greens: Add the cooked noodles and chopped spinach or bok choy to the pot. Simmer for a couple of minutes until everything is heated through. Season and Serve:
  • Season with salt and pepper according to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving. Feel free to customize by adding other vegetables, protein (like shredded chicken or tofu), or spices to suit your preferences. Thukpa is versatile and adaptable to various tastes.

Momos: Pillows of happiness, packed with flavorful surprises.

Facts

Fact 1: Tibetan Origin: Momos trace their roots to Tibetan cuisine, specifically originating from the Himalayan regions. They've evolved as a significant part of Tibetan culinary heritage, representing traditional flavors and cooking techniques.

Fact 2: Cultural Migration: Over time, momos spread across neighboring countries like Nepal, Bhutan, and India, adapting to local palates and becoming an integral part of their cuisines, showcasing cultural exchanges and culinary diversity.

Symbol of Community and Celebration: Momos are often associated with communal gatherings and festivities, commonly prepared during festive occasions or family gatherings, symbolizing unity and joyous celebrations in various Himalayan communities.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, etc.), finely chopped
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • A pinch of salt
  • For the Filling:
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar or lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe

  • Preparing the Dough: Mix the all-purpose flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth, pliable dough. Cover it and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  • Making the Filling: Heat oil in a pan. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté until fragrant. Add chopped onions and cook until translucent. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until they soften. Season with soy sauce, vinegar (or lemon juice), salt, and pepper. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  • Assembling the Momos:Divide the dough into small balls and roll each ball into a thin, round disc (about 3-4 inches in diameter). Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center of the disc. Fold and pleat the edges to seal the momos in desired shapes (half-moon, pleated circle, etc.). Steam the momos in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes until the outer covering becomes translucent and shiny. Serve hot with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, vinegar, chili sauce, or any preferred condiment. Adjust the filling ingredients and spices according to your taste preferences.

Skyu: A taste of Ladakhi heritage, filling hearts with every bite.

Facts

Fact 1: Traditional Ladakhi Dish: Skyu is a traditional staple dish originating from Ladakh, India, deeply rooted in the region's culinary heritage, and widely enjoyed by locals as a comforting meal.

Fact 2: Culinary Fusion: Reflecting historical exchanges, Skyu showcases a unique blend of Tibetan, Indian, and Central Asian culinary influences, incorporating local ingredients and cooking methods.

Fact 3: Community Cooking Tradition: Skyu holds cultural significance beyond its taste, often prepared and shared in communal gatherings, emphasizing unity and togetherness within Ladakhi communities through the preparation and enjoyment of this dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans), chopped
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or butter
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for cooking

Recipe

  1. Preparing the Dough: Take whole wheat flour in a large bowl. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes. Divide the dough into small, bite-sized balls. Roll each ball into a small disc shape.
  2. Making Skyu: Heat oil or butter in a large pan or pot. Add minced garlic and cumin seeds. Sauté until fragrant. Add chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Sprinkle turmeric powder and red chili powder. Stir well. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften. Place the prepared wheat flour discs (from step 1) into the pot, covering the vegetable mixture. Pour enough water to cover the wheat flour discs and vegetables. Cover the pot and cook on low-medium heat until the dough discs absorb the water and cook through, and the vegetables are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once cooked, Skyu should have a soft, dumpling-like texture with a mix of flavors from the vegetables and spices. Adjust the spices and vegetables according to your taste preferences. Serve hot and enjoy this hearty Ladakhi dish

Rogan Josh: A symphony of spices that sings on your palate.

Facts

Fact 1: Kashmiri Heritage Dish: Rogan Josh originates from Kashmiri cuisine, known for its aromatic red gravy and tender meat, reflecting the region's culinary legacy.

Fact 2: Distinctive Red Color:The dish gains its vibrant red hue from Kashmiri dry red chilies and other unique spices like fennel and saffron, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Fact 3: Slow Cooking Technique: Traditionally, Rogan Josh involves slow-cooking tenderized meat in aromatic spices, allowing flavors to meld, resulting in a flavorful and aromatic dish

Ingredients

  • 2-3 dry red Kashmiri chilies (or Kashmiri chili powder)
  • 500 grams mutton/lamb, cut into pieces
  • 3 tablespoons oil/ghee
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Marinating the Meat: Marinate the meat with yogurt (1 cup) and salt for at least 1-2 hours or overnight for enhanced flavor (optional).
  2. Step 2: Cooking Rogan Josh: Heat oil/ghee in a pan or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, slit green chilies, and dry red Kashmiri chilies (or Kashmiri chili powder). Sauté for a couple of minutes. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, ground coriander, and ground ginger. Mix well and cook the spices for a minute. Add the marinated meat (if marinated) or the raw meat pieces to the pan. Stir and cook until the meat changes color. Pour in tomato puree or chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate. Add water (as needed), cover the pan or pressure cooker, and cook until the meat is tender. Adjust the consistency of the gravy as desired. Add saffron strands (if using) and simmer for a few more minutes.
  3. Step 3: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving. Adjust spices according to taste preferences. This aromatic Rogan Josh pairs excellently with steamed rice or naan, offering a delightful taste of Kashmiri cuisine.

Lotus Root: Nature's intricate puzzle, crunchy and delightful

Facts

Fact 1: Ancient Asian Staple: Lotus root, a culinary mainstay in Asia, has been cherished for centuries in Chinese and Japanese cuisine for its crunchy texture and mild flavor.

Fact 2: Symbol of Significance: Beyond the kitchen, it holds cultural symbolism, representing purity and longevity, mirroring the revered lotus flower's symbolism in Asian cultures.

Fact 3: Medicinal Value: Esteemed in traditional medicine, lotus root is believed to offer health benefits, aiding digestion, fortifying the respiratory system, and promoting well-being.

Ingredients

  • Optional: Chili flakes or sesame seeds for garnish
  • 1 lotus root (about 300-400g)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Prepare Lotus Root: Peel the lotus root and slice it thinly. You can also soak it in water with a bit of vinegar to prevent discoloration.
  2. Step 2: Cooking Process: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, sauté for a minute until fragrant. Add the sliced lotus root to the pan, stir well, and cook for about 5-7 minutes until it starts to soften.
  3. Step 3: Seasoning: Pour soy sauce, rice vinegar, and honey (or sugar) over the lotus root. Stir and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the lotus root slices are tender yet slightly crispy.
  4. Step 4: Finishing Touch: Adjust seasoning with salt if needed. Garnish with chili flakes or sesame seeds if desired.
  5. Step 5: Serve: Serve the lotus root as a side dish or as part of a stir-fry with other vegetables or proteins. Adjust the seasoning and flavors according to your taste preferences. This simple recipe highlights the natural crispness and delicate taste of lotus root
