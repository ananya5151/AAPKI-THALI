Rajma Chawal: A comforting hug in every bite!

Facts

Fact 1: Columbian Exchange Influence: Rajma's introduction to India during the Columbian Exchange in the 16th century marked its integration into North Indian cuisine, evolving over time to become the beloved Rajma Chawal.

Fact 2: Cultural Comfort Food: Beyond its culinary appeal, Rajma Chawal represents a cultural comfort food, often associated with familial gatherings, simplicity, and the warmth of home-cooked meals.

Fact 3: Regional Adaptations: Across India, Rajma Chawal varies regionally, with diverse adaptations and unique spins on the recipe, showcasing the culinary diversity and local flavors embraced within this beloved dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil/ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

  • Preparing Rajma Masala: Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Saute for a few seconds. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Saute for a minute. Add tomato puree or finely chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and oil starts to separate. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  • Combining Rajma and Masala: Add the cooked kidney beans (rajma) along with its water to the masala. Mix and bring to a simmer. Add salt to taste. Adjust consistency by adding more water if needed. Simmer the rajma masala on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  • Making Steamed Rice (Chawal): Rinse basmati rice until the water runs clear. Cook the rice in a pot with water (use 1.5 to 2 cups of water per cup of rice) until it's fluffy and fully cooked.
  • Serving: Serve the hot and flavorful Rajma with steamed rice. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve alongside some yogurt, salad, or pickle for a complete meal. Adjust spices and quantities according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your homemade Rajma Chawal!

Dum Aloo: Spiced perfection in every potato bite

Facts

Fact 1: Roots in Kashmiri Cuisine: Dum Aloo originates from Kashmiri cuisine, believed to have been introduced by the Mughals. The dish traditionally features small potatoes cooked in a slow-cooked, flavorful gravy, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of the region..

Fact 2: Evolution of Cooking Technique: The term 'Dum' refers to the slow-cooking method involving sealing the pot with dough or a tight-fitting lid to trap steam. This technique allows the potatoes to absorb the spices and flavors, resulting in a rich, aromatic dish..

Fact 3: Adaptation and Variations:Over time, Dum Aloo has seen various adaptations across different regions of India. Each adaptation incorporates local spices, cooking methods, and ingredients, resulting in diverse versions that reflect the cultural diversity of Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams baby potatoes (or regular potatoes cut into quarters)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil/ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

  • Preparing Potatoes: Peel the baby potatoes or cut regular potatoes into quarters. Prick them with a fork or make shallow cuts to allow flavors to penetrate. Parboil the potatoes in salted water for about 5-7 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  • Making Dum Aloo: Heat oil/ghee in a pan or pot. Add the parboiled potatoes and fry them until they turn golden brown. Remove and set aside. In the same pan, add more oil if needed. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Saute for a minute.Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Add tomato puree or finely chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the mixture thickens. Lower the heat and add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, dry mango powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes. Whisk the plain yogurt well and gradually add it to the mixture while stirring continuously. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala. Add the fried potatoes to the masala. Mix gently to coat the potatoes with the masala. Add some water (as needed for the desired consistency), cover with a lid, and let it simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  • Serving: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice, naan, or roti. Adjust the spices and quantities according to your taste preferences. Dum Aloo is known for its rich and flavorful gravy, making it a delightful dish in Indian cuisine.

Goshtaba: Tender, flavorful bites from the heart of Kashmiri cuisine.

Facts

Fact 1: Mughal Culinary Legacy: Goshtaba traces its roots to the Mughal kitchens, reflecting a dish crafted for royalty, featuring delicate meatballs cooked in a yogurt-based gravy, showcasing the finesse of Mughlai cuisine.

Fact 2: Traditional Kashmiri Feast: This dish is a prominent part of Kashmiri Wazwan, a multi-course meal served during weddings and special occasions, highlighting the cultural significance and richness of Kashmiri culinary traditions.

Fact 3: Unique Flavor Profile: Goshtaba's preparation involves meticulous pounding of meat with spices, resulting in soft, flavorful meatballs simmered in a creamy yogurt gravy, encapsulating the essence of Kashmir's gastronomic heritage.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 tablespoons almond paste (optional)
  • 500 grams minced mutton/lamb
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2-3 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1 teaspoon dry ginger powder (saunth)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil/ghee
  • For the Gravy:
  • 2 cups yogurt
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 black cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil/ghee

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Making Meatballs (Goshtaba): In a bowl, combine minced meat, chopped onions, green chilies, gram flour, fennel seeds, ginger powder, dry ginger powder, cumin powder, cardamom powder, saffron strands (if using), and salt. Mix the ingredients thoroughly and shape the mixture into medium-sized meatballs.
  2. Step 2: Cooking Meatballs: Heat oil/ghee in a pan and fry the meatballs until they turn golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. Step 3: Preparing Gravy: In another pan, heat oil/ghee. Add black cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, fennel seeds, and cumin seeds. Let them splutter. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Saute until aromatic. Whisk yogurt and almond paste (if using) in a separate bowl and gradually add it to the pan while stirring continuously. Add salt to taste and simmer the gravy for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.
  4. Step 4: Simmering Goshtaba in Gravy: Add the fried meatballs (Goshtaba) to the gravy and let them simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  5. Step 5: Serving: Goshtaba is typically served hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves or a sprinkle of saffron, alongside steamed rice or naan. Adjust spices and quantities according to your taste preferences. Goshtaba's preparation demands patience and attention to detail, resulting in a flavorful and aromatic Kashmiri delicacy.

Rogan Josh: A symphony of spices that sings on your palate.

Facts

Fact 1: Kashmiri Heritage Dish: Rogan Josh originates from Kashmiri cuisine, known for its aromatic red gravy and tender meat, reflecting the region's culinary legacy.

Fact 2: Distinctive Red Color:The dish gains its vibrant red hue from Kashmiri dry red chilies and other unique spices like fennel and saffron, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Fact 3: Slow Cooking Technique: Traditionally, Rogan Josh involves slow-cooking tenderized meat in aromatic spices, allowing flavors to meld, resulting in a flavorful and aromatic dish

Ingredients

  • 2-3 dry red Kashmiri chilies (or Kashmiri chili powder)
  • 500 grams mutton/lamb, cut into pieces
  • 3 tablespoons oil/ghee
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Marinating the Meat: Marinate the meat with yogurt (1 cup) and salt for at least 1-2 hours or overnight for enhanced flavor (optional).
  2. Step 2: Cooking Rogan Josh: Heat oil/ghee in a pan or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, slit green chilies, and dry red Kashmiri chilies (or Kashmiri chili powder). Sauté for a couple of minutes. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, ground coriander, and ground ginger. Mix well and cook the spices for a minute. Add the marinated meat (if marinated) or the raw meat pieces to the pan. Stir and cook until the meat changes color. Pour in tomato puree or chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate. Add water (as needed), cover the pan or pressure cooker, and cook until the meat is tender. Adjust the consistency of the gravy as desired. Add saffron strands (if using) and simmer for a few more minutes.
  3. Step 3: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving. Adjust spices according to taste preferences. This aromatic Rogan Josh pairs excellently with steamed rice or naan, offering a delightful taste of Kashmiri cuisine.

Lotus Root: Nature's intricate puzzle, crunchy and delightful

Facts

Fact 1: Ancient Asian Staple: Lotus root, a culinary mainstay in Asia, has been cherished for centuries in Chinese and Japanese cuisine for its crunchy texture and mild flavor.

Fact 2: Symbol of Significance: Beyond the kitchen, it holds cultural symbolism, representing purity and longevity, mirroring the revered lotus flower's symbolism in Asian cultures.

Fact 3: Medicinal Value: Esteemed in traditional medicine, lotus root is believed to offer health benefits, aiding digestion, fortifying the respiratory system, and promoting well-being.

Ingredients

  • Optional: Chili flakes or sesame seeds for garnish
  • 1 lotus root (about 300-400g)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • Salt to taste

Recipe

  1. Step 1: Prepare Lotus Root: Peel the lotus root and slice it thinly. You can also soak it in water with a bit of vinegar to prevent discoloration.
  2. Step 2: Cooking Process: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, sauté for a minute until fragrant. Add the sliced lotus root to the pan, stir well, and cook for about 5-7 minutes until it starts to soften.
  3. Step 3: Seasoning: Pour soy sauce, rice vinegar, and honey (or sugar) over the lotus root. Stir and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the lotus root slices are tender yet slightly crispy.
  4. Step 4: Finishing Touch: Adjust seasoning with salt if needed. Garnish with chili flakes or sesame seeds if desired.
  5. Step 5: Serve: Serve the lotus root as a side dish or as part of a stir-fry with other vegetables or proteins. Adjust the seasoning and flavors according to your taste preferences. This simple recipe highlights the natural crispness and delicate taste of lotus root
